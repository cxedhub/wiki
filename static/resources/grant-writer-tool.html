4 Step 4 Submit to the Funder External

Where the rubber meets the road

Most funders require submission through their own portal — we can't auto-submit for you. Here's a quick checklist before you hit send:

  • Re-read the funder's RFP / guidelines side-by-side with your draft. Did you address every required section?
  • Check page or character limits. Trim ruthlessly.
  • Verify every dollar amount, deadline, citation, and statistic. AI tools hallucinate — you're accountable for every word.
  • Get one human reader (a colleague, your principal, your district grants office) to skim before submission.
  • Gather required attachments: letters of support, IRS determination letter (for school nonprofits), W-9, principal sign-off, district pre-approval.
  • Save your final .md draft locally — you'll re-use 80% of it for the next funder.

When you're ready, head to your target funder's portal (or the Find a Grant directory below to pick one).

📚 Find a Grant — directory of common K-12 STEM/CS funders

Starting points if you haven't picked a funder yet. Always confirm current eligibility, deadlines, and award amounts on each funder's official site — programs change yearly.

Crowdfunding (lowest barrier — start here for small classroom requests)
  • DonorsChoose
    Public schoolAny subjectPost a project, individual donors fund it. Excellent for $100–$2,000 hardware buys (micro:bits, sensors, books).
  • AdoptAClassroom.org
    PreK-12Flexible spendTeachers receive funds via gift-card-style account; spend at participating retailers.
National foundations & education non-profits
  • NEA Foundation Educator Grants
    All grade bandsUp to ~$5,000Learning & Leadership grants and Student Success grants — multiple deadlines per year.
  • Toshiba America Foundation
    K-12STEMK–5 (small grants) and 6–12 (up to $5,000+); favors hands-on, classroom-led projects.
  • McCarthey Dressman Education Foundation
    K-12Teacher-ledMulti-year teacher development grants for innovative, replicable classroom projects.
  • Society for Science STEM Action Grants
    Middle/HighResearchFor middle & high school science research and outreach.
Industry / corporate education programs
  • Walmart Local Community Grants
    Local school$250–$5,000Apply through your local store. Fast-turnaround small grants.
  • Lowe's Toolbox for Education / 100 Hometowns
    SchoolsMaker spacesBuilding, makerspace, and STEM lab support — eligibility and program names rotate yearly.
  • Captain Planet Foundation
    PreK-12EnvironmentalProject-based, student-led environmental STEM. Great fit for sensor / IoT data-collection projects.
Computer science / micro:bit-specific opportunities
  • micro:bit Educational Foundation — do your :bit
    GlobalChallengeAnnual student challenge tied to UN Sustainable Development Goals; recognition + occasional micro:bit hardware support.
  • Code.org Regional Partner network
    K-12 CSPDFree PD pathways and curriculum for CS Fundamentals, CS Discoveries, CS Principles. Often subsidized in your region.
  • CSTA Grants & Awards
    MembersCS-specificConference scholarships, classroom mini-grants, and recognition awards through the Computer Science Teachers Association.
  • Lemelson-MIT InvenTeam
    High schoolUp to $7,500For student teams inventing technological solutions to real-world problems.
Federal & state pathways (work through your district)
  • Title I, Part A (ESSA)
    High-needDistrict-routedTalk to your district's federal programs office about using Title I set-asides for STEM tech.
  • Title IV, Part A — Student Support & Academic Enrichment
    Tech allowedDistrict-routedDesigned in part for STEM, well-rounded education, and ed-tech. Your district has an allocation.
  • FCC E-Rate
    ConnectivityDistrict-routedIf your project requires school connectivity upgrades, your district may already have an active E-Rate filing.
  • NSF education programs (DRK-12, ITEST, CSforAll)
    Research-gradeUniversity partnerLarger awards, almost always require a higher-ed or research partner. Reach out to your nearest university school of education.
Local & regional starting points
  • State department of education
    VariesSearch your state DoE site for teacher mini-grants, CS for All allocations, perkins funds, or innovation grants.
  • Local community foundations
    LocalSearch "[Your County/City] Community Foundation grants" — many have an education-focused fund with rolling deadlines.
  • Local employers, utilities, credit unions
    LocalOften $250–$2,500 with a short application. Best ROI on writing time.
  • PTA / PTO mini-grants
    BuildingThe fastest, easiest "first grant" for many teachers. Ask your PTA/PTO board.

This directory is for orientation only — eligibility, award amounts, and deadlines change every year. Always verify on the funder's official site before applying. Listing here is not an endorsement, and absence does not mean a funder is bad. Use the LLM iteration in Step 2 to brainstorm additional, locally-relevant funders for your specific context.
